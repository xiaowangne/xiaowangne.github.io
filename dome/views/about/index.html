Kojima, the birthplace of the domestically-manufactured jeans in Japan... In 1962, Betty Smith Company was established in Kojima as the first domestic manufacturer of lady´s jeans in Japan.

The Beginning

“In 1962, my father established a company called Oshima Clothing, he built this factory in the same year. In 1965, BIG JOHN started manufacturing the first blue jeans commercially in Japan. As Betty Smith and BIG JOHN were family partners, we ended up joining forces to produce blue jeans. Around that time, there were only men´s jeans being sold. But they were still selling well. Women would wear men´s jeans and they were fine with it. However, jeans cut for men didn´t really flatter a women´s physique. Then we came up with the idea to specialize in women´s jeans. In 1970, we launched the Betty Smith brand. That´s how we started as the first women´s jeans brand in Japan.”

- Quoted from 大島康弘 ( the President of Betty Smith )

Betty Smith Jeans Museum & Village

In 2003, “Jeans Museum” as the first jeans museum in Japan was opened. Betty Smith has also been continuously proposing new values to the jeans industry for the past half century while it took an initiative to start manufacturing the world’s first “Fully custom-made jeans” in the same year. The spirit of Betty Smith as always challenging for a new endeavor will not be changed at all from now on.
At Betty Smith, it defines “Creating a Japanese jeans culture” as a corporate mission, then we certainly would like to actively create new values for the next half century on a continuous basis.
